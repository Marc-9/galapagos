Fun Facts

  1. The Galapagos Islands have the last living Giant Tortoise on Earth. He is over 80 years old and even though scientists attemted to have him fertilize an egg they were unsuccessful.

  2. Along with the Ginat Tortoise the Galapagos islands also have the Scolopendra Centipede which can be over 30 centimeters long and feeds on Rats and Lizards.

  3. The convergence of four ocean currents including Equatorial, Cromwell, Humboldt, and Panama has resulted in the unpredictability of the climate of the Islands.

  4. The Marine Iguanas found in the Islands are the only known lizards that can swim.

  5. The Darwinian Theory of the Origin of Man was mostly fomented by the study of the Galapagos Islands.

  6. The Galapagos is home to the only breeding penguin in the northern hemisphere.

  7. There are over 400 types of fishes on the galapagos, over 50 of them are only at the Galapagos islands.
